Projects & Contributions

Below are representative examples of my design work in robotics, radar sensing, sealing, thermal management, and manufacturability. (Some CAD is simplified or redacted for confidentiality.)

Autonomous Mobile Robot

Sensor & LiDAR Mount Architecture

Designed and released precision mounting hardware for LiDAR and camera perception stacks on an AMR platform. Goals: vibration stability, maintain alignment, allow field service, and stay manufacturable.

Highlights:
• Integrated multi-sensor stack with defined datums and tolerance control
• Defined key torque values for fasteners using engineering references.
• Balanced stiffness vs. weight vs. machinability

Ruggedized Radar

Novel Ground-Positioning Radar Design

Led the mechanical design for an environmental housing protecting RF hardware mounted underneath autonomous vehicles for localization. Managed sealing strategy, connector strain relief, environmental loads (shock, vibration, thermal, corrosion), and manufacturability through vendors.

Highlights:
• IP-style sealing approach for road salt, debris, splash
• Thermal conduction path from high-power electronics to exterior surfaces
• DFMA: minimized fastener count and simplified assembly order

Manufacturing Tooling

Assembly / Test Fixtures

Built fixtures and corresponding work instructions to ensure repeatable assembly, reduce handling damage, and lock in QC checkpoints. Captured assembly know-how into a documented, controlled process.

Highlights:
• Reduced alignment variability across operators
• Supported traceability and PLM-controlled revision of process docs
• Helped scale from prototype bench builds to an assembly line environment
